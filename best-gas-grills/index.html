The best gas grills of 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

For most people, the Weber Spirit II E-310 offers the best balance of cooking performance, build quality, and long-term value in a three-burner propane grill.

The Weber Genesis II E-310 is the top choice if you want premium materials and searing power, while the Char-Broil Performance 300 is the best budget-friendly pick for occasional grilling.

Gas grills remain the most convenient way to get that backyard barbecue flavor without the wait and fuss of charcoal. In 2026, the market is split between solid three-burner workhorses and larger four-burner setups, with most improvements focused on even heat distribution and easier cleanup. When choosing, pay attention to burner count (three is plenty for a family of four; four gives you more zone control), cooking area shape and size, and how well the lid seals. Build quality varies wildly at different price points, and a heavier gauge lid or stainless steel body usually means better heat retention and longer life. Propane is still king for portability and instant-on convenience, but if you have natural gas available at home, many of these models can be converted. The key is to match the grill to your cooking volume: occasional burger flippers can save money with a budget model, while weekly entertainers will appreciate the even heat and sturdy construction of a premium grill. Below we break down five top contenders, from the no-compromise Weber Genesis to the surprisingly capable Nexgrill four-burner.

Best for specific needs

Best for everyday family grilling

If you fire up the grill three or more times a week for burgers, chicken, and veggies, the Weber Spirit II E-310 is the obvious choice. Its even heat, reliable ignition, and robust build mean you’ll spend less time worrying and more time cooking. The three burners give enough cooking area for a family of five, and the porcelain-enameled exterior wipes clean with a damp cloth. Owners consistently report ten-year-plus lifespans with basic care, which is rare at this price tier. Our pick: Weber Spirit II E-310.

Best for budget-conscious beginners

For new grillers or anyone who wants to keep the initial investment low, the Char-Broil Performance 300 delivers a genuine three-burner propane grill for a fraction of the cost of premium models. It won’t win any durability awards, but it will cook burgers and steaks just fine for a few years. Add a cheap grill cover and store it in a dry spot, and you can stretch its lifespan. If the outdoor cooking bug bites you, you can always upgrade later without feeling like you wasted a lot of money. Our pick: Char-Broil Performance 300.

Best for large gatherings and versatility

When you’re cooking for a crowd, the Nexgrill 720-0830H’s four burners and side burner give you the ability to grill mains, simmer sauces, and even run a rotisserie simultaneously. The extra real estate means you can fit 24 burgers or a couple of whole chickens without crowding. It’s not as well-built as a Weber, but the feature set rivals grills costing hundreds more. If you host parties a few times a month and want maximum utility per dollar, this is your grill. Our pick: Nexgrill 720-0830H.

Our verdict

Propane gas grills in 2026 offer more choices than ever, but the right one for you depends on how often you cook, your climate, and your budget. The Weber Spirit II E-310 remains the best all-around purchase for most households, delivering reliable performance and longevity without the sticker shock of the premium Genesis line. If you need a four-burner layout for large parties, the Nexgrill 720-0830H provides the best value in that segment. For infrequent use or a very tight budget, the Char-Broil Performance 300 gets the job done. Whichever you pick, invest in a good cover and store it out of the rain, and your grill will reward you with many memorable meals.
